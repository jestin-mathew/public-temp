TRENDS IN INDIA’S FINTECH SECTOR 2023:

WHAT TO LOOK FORWARD TO?

India continues to emerge as a global leader in fintech thanks to the country’s booming startup ecosystem and emerging companies that provide innovative financial services right from online payments to lending and insurance.

Fintech has revolutionized the Indian financial sector through smart technologies like neobanks, digital lending platforms, etc. Fintech companies in India continue to churn out amazing financial products with the help of technology like artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, etc to provide innovative solutions.

Even though fintech has been around for quite a while, the sudden increase in its adoption was mainly due to the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic. The absence of access to physical financial services during this period forced many into adopting digital financial services. The simplicity, speed and efficiency of these digital services have made people find affection towards them. This has led them to prefer digital services even after the pandemic period had ceased, contributing to growth in the fintech space.

With the exponential growth in the number of people adopting fintech, players in the field have ventured into new opportunities like wealth, insurance, etc in a bid to come up with more exciting product offerings to entice their customers.

The following are a few fintech trends that India is likely to witness in the year 2023:

AI in digital lending

IAI or artificial intelligence has always been a topic of fascination. It has found use in several industries and helps simplify tasks. Artificial intelligence can be effectively put to use, especially in digital lending.

Many financial institutions have collaborated with fintech players to improve their AI capabilities which might result in useful insights that can be used to strengthen their brands. It can help reduce delays in loan disbursals and speed up the process minimizing bias.

Neobanks

Neobanks are another innovative technology that has revamped the Indian financial sector. These are banks that have an online presence and they operate on cloud computing. Due to India’s growing adoption of digital financial services, many neobank players have found interest in the country.

One main attraction of neobanks is that they make use of machine learning and data analytics. They also provide customized offerings depending on various customer segments. Many traditional banks are now partnering with neobanks to widen their access to customers. This partnership benefits banks in terms of accessibility to latest financial technology and helps create hyper-focused products in lending, payments, cards, etc.

With the introduction of embedded banking in India, neobanks have a great opportunity for growth.

Insurtech

Just like how the banking sector has undergone a technological revamp, insurance companies have partnered with tech companies to come up with innovative products that appeal to customers. With the help of technology like AI, machine learning and blockchain, insurance companies are rolling out products like embedded insurance, bite-sized insurance schemes, etc to attract prospective customers.

Many insurtech startups are digitizing claim processes with the help of robotic automation and language processing. Many even offer cyber security insurance that protects customers from cyber attacks like hacking, phishing, identity theft, etc.

Metaverse

The metaverse is an upcoming technology that has the potential to change the way different industries work, including fintech. A popular aspect of the metaverse is avatars. They come in different forms and are designed to represent real-world people in the metaverse. These avatars are increasingly becoming more realistic.

There is an option to purchase NFTs to use as avatars in the metaverse. These NFTs are linked to the blockchain as a separate entity.

Robo advisory services

Innovations in the field of artificial intelligence have enabled investors to obtain computerized financial guidance. These advisors can help analyze large volumes of data and adjust to changing environments faster. They are especially helpful for those new investors who don’t have proper access to traditional financial counseling.

Digital currency

India would soon be launching its own digital currency in 2023. Also known as CBDC, the country is launching its own digital currency with the intent to make faster, cheaper and easier transactions. The CBDC currency will be regulated by the RBI

Reg Tech

Reg Tech or regulatory technology can be described as the technology that is used for improving regulatory and compliance processes. This is important in helping ensure that fintech companies comply with industry regulations. Reg Tech is highly relevant in 2023 and is slated to be one of the important fintech trends to watch out for this year.

Cybersecurity

As the world is leaning towards a completely digital world, instances of online fraud become more predominant. In 2023, it would be crucial to emphasize a strong underlying risk infrastructure that helps in protecting user data by creating a smooth experience for the user. This will help in improving innovation and scalability.

INTO THE WORLD OF THE FINTECH SANDBOX:

WHAT IS ALL THE HYPE ABOUT?

India has been a growing hub for fintech innovation. The rate at which fintech startups are cropping up is proof enough of this fact. With several fintech organizations around, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has constituted a regulatory sandbox to monitor the rapidly changing fintech environment. The objective behind the fintech sandbox was to ensure that entities like fintech organizations are subject to regulations just like banking institutions to ensure transparency and incentivize innovations.

What is a fintech regulatory sandbox?

The regulatory sandbox is a live testing environment for fintech participants to test their products/services in a regulated environment. It enables regulators, organizations and customers to conduct field tests to identify both the benefits and risks of new innovative fintech products while ensuring to monitor and contain the possible risks associated with it. It can be referred to as an essential tool that enables the development of a dynamic environment that evolves with emerging technologies.

Any fintech product/service intended for launching needs processing with the help of new or emerging technologies and should address the common problems faced by customers.

What is the purpose of a regulatory sandbox?

A regulatory sandbox provides a real-life testing environment for market participants to test their new innovative products/services. They identify the risks and opportunities carried out by some innovations and help develop a suitable environment that accommodates them.

Despite the innovativeness of the product/service, they must fit within the purview of the existing legal framework. Apart from ensuring that new products/services follow the regulations, it also helps legislators gain the required knowledge to formulate the required regulatory policies in the future. This is done to ensure that the regulatory sandbox keeps up with the pace of digitalization.

What is the process of the regulatory sandbox?

The testing of fintech products/services in the sandbox will be under the guidance of the Inter-Departmental Group of the RBI with participation by experts in the domain.

The sandbox follows a 5 step process:

  1. Initial screening
    Applications are screened and applicants are shortlisted based on eligibility criteria. The selected applicants need to understand the objectives of the sandbox and conform to them.
  2. Testing the design
    The design is tested through an iterative engagement process and the metrics for evaluating outcomes and risks will be identified.
  3. Assessment of the application
    The design is vetted by the regulatory group and regulatory modifications will be suggested if there are any.
  4. Testing
    Empirical evidence will be generated to closely monitor and assess the design tests.
  5. Evaluation
    The final testing outcome will be determined by the RBI based on whether the product/service meets the criteria specified by the regulatory sandbox.
What are the benefits of a fintech regulatory sandbox?

Some important benefits of regulatory sandboxes are as follows:

  • Serves as an opportunity to test viability of the product
    It aids in the testing of the product/service without the need for expensive roll-outs. This helps firms to adequately test the product before launching them in the final market space.
  • Reduced dependence on stakeholder consultation
    Since the sandbox consists of a structured environment, the need to depend on stakeholders for consultation purposes is reduced to a great extent.
  • Helps increase the quality of fintech products and services
    As the new innovations of fintech players are tested in a well-monitored environment subject to regulatory requirements, it will help provide quality products/services to the end consumer.
  • Aids in the formation of partnerships
    The sandbox environment enables fintech organizations to collaborate with other fintech firms to share technology and resources.
    • Are regulatory sandboxes effective?

    Sandboxes are essential in helping ensure new fintech products/services follow the appropriate legal framework. It helps in improving both the pace of innovation and financial reach. Sectors such as microfinance, mobile banking and remittances greatly benefit from regulatory sandboxes.

    Why is fintech regulation necessary?

    Fintech has played a huge role in transforming the financial services landscape. As fintech continues to spread and prosper, it is only natural that it is prone to risks. Hence, it is important to have regulations in place as it signifies trust. It helps protect consumers against fraud and secures payments. Regulation ensures that only quality products/services are delivered to the customer.
